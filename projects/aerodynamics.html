A computational aerodynamics analysis tool for finite wing performance

This MATLAB implementation provides comprehensive finite wing aerodynamics analysis using panel methods. The solver calculates lift distributions, induced velocities, and effective angles of attack, with specialized capabilities for modeling propeller-wing interactions and their effects on wing performance.

Key Features

Panel Method Solver

Discretized wing span analysis using vortex panel methods to calculate circulation distribution and induced velocities

Propeller-Wing Interaction

Analysis of propeller slipstream effects on wing performance, including velocity augmentation in propeller wake regions

Lift Distribution Analysis

Spanwise lift coefficient and circulation distribution calculations with iterative convergence to steady-state solutions

Comparative Studies

Side-by-side comparison of wing performance with and without propeller effects to quantify interaction benefits

Demonstrated Use Case: Propeller-Wing Interaction Analysis

The Mission

Analyze the aerodynamic performance of a finite wing with and without propeller effects to understand how propeller slipstream enhances lift generation and affects spanwise lift distribution.

Model Methodology

Panel Method

The wing is discretized into panels along the span, with each panel represented by a bound vortex. The trailing vortices extend downstream, creating an induced velocity field that affects the effective angle of attack at each panel location.

Propeller Effects

Propeller slipstream creates an accelerated flow region behind the propeller disk. The velocity augmentation is calculated using actuator disk theory, where the velocity behind the propeller is determined by the thrust, propeller radius, and freestream conditions.

Numerical Solution

The solution is obtained through an iterative process:

  • Initialization: Start with zero induced velocity assumption
  • Circulation Calculation: Compute bound vortex strength from effective angle of attack and lift coefficient
  • Induced Velocity: Calculate induced velocity at each panel from trailing vortices
  • Convergence: Iterate until circulation distribution converges to steady-state

Results

The analysis reveals significant differences in lift distribution between cases with and without propeller effects. The propeller slipstream increases local velocities in the wake region, leading to enhanced lift generation and modified spanwise lift distribution.

Model Efficacy

The panel method implementation successfully captures the essential physics of finite wing aerodynamics, including three-dimensional effects and propeller-wing interactions. The iterative solver converges to stable solutions, providing accurate lift distributions and performance metrics.

Future Work

Future enhancements could include unsteady aerodynamics, compressibility effects, and more sophisticated propeller models incorporating rotational effects and non-uniform slipstream distributions.
